Product Explanation

Earicular is a senior design capstone website that showcases our research and design work on auricular reconstruction. The project combines biomaterials, 3D modeling, and clinical planning to create a more accessible pathway for patients with microtia, traumatic ear loss, and congenital deformities.

Research-backed design

Needs Statement

Individuals with ear deformities require reconstructive solutions that are affordable, cosmetically satisfactory, and clinically repeatable. Current options are either highly invasive, difficult to scale, or rely on custom surgical expertise that is not widely available.

  • Patient need: a practical reconstructive pathway for microtia and traumatic ear loss.
  • Clinical need: a method that reduces surgeon variability and improves outcomes.
  • Market need: a solution that fits into healthcare workflows and can be adopted by clinics.

Competitor Analysis

We compare existing auricular reconstruction approaches, highlighting the strengths and gaps in current solutions.

Autologous Reconstruction

Uses rib cartilage grafts and requires multiple surgeries. It is durable but highly invasive and surgeon-dependent.

Silicone Prosthetics

Provides good aesthetics, but the prosthesis must be replaced periodically and can be uncomfortable for the patient.

3D Bioprinting & Scaffolds

Emerging technologies promise customization, but they are not yet widely available and face regulatory hurdles.

Market Strategy

Our strategy emphasizes partnerships, clinical validation, and clear communication to key stakeholders.

  1. Clinical partners: collaborate with reconstructive surgeons and craniofacial clinics.
  2. Academic adoption: share findings and workflows with biomedical engineering programs.
  3. Patient outreach: present the benefits of our approach to advocacy groups and families.
